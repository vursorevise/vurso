2. A note for younger users

Vurso is used by GCSE and A-Level students, many of whom are under 18. We've tried to design this policy — and the Service itself — in line with the ICO's Age Appropriate Design Code: we collect the minimum data needed to run the Service, we don't use your data for targeted advertising, and account settings default to the most private option. If you're a parent or guardian with questions about your child's account, contact us using the details above.

4. How we use your data

  • To provide and operate the Service, including generating your study content via AI;
  • To manage your account, authentication, and subscription billing;
  • To enforce free-plan usage limits and prevent abuse;
  • To fix bugs, understand usage, and improve the Service;
  • To contact you about your account, transactional emails (e.g. payment receipts), or important changes to the Service;
  • To comply with our legal obligations (e.g. tax records for payments).

We do not sell your personal data, and we do not use your study notes or essays to serve you advertising.

5. Legal basis for processing (UK GDPR)

  • Contract: processing needed to provide the Service you've signed up to (account, generations, subscription).
  • Legitimate interests: keeping the Service secure, preventing abuse of free-tier limits, and improving the product — balanced against your right to privacy.
  • Consent: where we ask for it separately (e.g. optional marketing emails, if we ever introduce these).
  • Legal obligation: for things like retaining payment records for tax purposes.

  • Account & profile data: kept while your account is active, and deleted within [X] days of account deletion.

8. Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you;
  • Ask us to correct inaccurate data;
  • Ask us to delete your data ("right to erasure") — you can request full account deletion at any time;
  • Ask us to restrict or object to certain processing;
  • Receive your data in a portable format;
  • Withdraw consent at any time, where we rely on consent;
  • Complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk if you think we've mishandled your data.

To exercise any of these rights, email [privacy/support email]. We'll respond within one month.

9. Security

We use industry-standard measures to protect your data, including encrypted connections (HTTPS), hashed passwords, and access controls on our database. No system is 100% secure, but we work to keep your information safe and will notify you and the ICO of any data breach where required by law.

10. Cookies & local storage

Vurso uses essential cookies/local storage to keep you signed in, remember your theme and study preferences, and track your session locally on your device (e.g. streaks, saved timetable). We don't use third-party advertising or tracking cookies.
